Banana Bread Serves 10 (Makes one 9 inch loaf)

Description

Are you ready to taste the best banana bread you've ever had? This recipe calls for extracting and reducing the liquid in the bananas to provide all the banana flavor without adding extra liquid to the mixture. This recipe uses butter and brown sugar to add a deep nutty richness and uses slices of banana and sugar on top to provide a carmalized richness and crunch to the top.

Ingredients:

  • 1 3/4 cups (8 3/4 oz) unbleached all-purpose-flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp table salt
  • 6 very ripe bananas
  • 8 tbsp unsalted butter (one stick), melted and slightly cooled
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3/4 cups (5 1/4 oz) packed light-brown sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup walnuts, toasted and coarsely chopped (optional)
  • 2 tsp granulated sugar

Steps

  1. Adjust oven rack to middle position and preheat oven to 350 degrees. Spray 9 inch loaf pan with non-stick cooking spray. Whisk flour, baking soda, and salt together in a large bowl.
  2. Place 5 bananas in microwave-safe bowl; cover with plastic wrap and cut several steam vents into plastic with a paring knife. Microwave on high power until bananas are soft and have released liquid, about 5 minutes. Transfer bananas to a fine-mesh strainer placed over a medium bowl and allow to drain, stirring occasionally, 15 minutes. This should produce 1/2 to 3/4 cups of liquid.
  3. Transfer liquid to a medium saucepan and cook over medium-high heat until reduced to 1/4 cup, about 5 minutes. Remove pan from the heat, stir reduced liquid into the bananas, and mash with a potato masher until fairly smooth. Whisk in butter, eggs, brown sugar, and vanilla.
  4. Pour banana mixture into flour mixture and stir until just combined with some streaks of flour remaining. Gently fold in walnuts, if using. Scrape batter into prepared pan. Slice remaining banana diagonally into 1/4" thick slices. Leaving 1 1/2" space down the center, place banana slices down the top of both sides of the loaf. Sprinkle granulated sugar evenly over loaf.
  5. Bake until toothpick inserted in the center of loaf comes out clean, 55 to 75 minutes. Cool bread in pan on wire rack for 15 minutes, then remove loaf from pan and continue to cook on wire rack. Enjoy warm or at room temperature.
